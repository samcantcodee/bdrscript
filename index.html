Step 1
Open
"Hi, is this [company name]?" Wait for yes. Then say:
"Can I speak with the owner please?" If they ask why, say:
"I was looking at their reviews online. I have a quick question for them."
Step 2
Butter them up
"Hi [name], my name is Sherwin. I am calling from GridOps. I was on your Google reviews before calling you." Pause 1 second. Then:
"Your clients are saying really great things about your service." ⏸ Pause
"They talk about how professional your drivers are. How clean your vehicles are. How on time you always are." ⏸ Pause
"And [John] is getting mentioned by name in a few reviews. That does not happen often. That is a really big deal." Pause. Let them react. Wait for them to say thank you.
Step 3
The pivot
"The only thing that keeps coming up in the negative reviews has nothing to do with your service." ⏸ Pause
"It is always about the booking experience."
Step 4
Hard hitting question
"Have you ever thought about digitalizing your booking?" Stop talking completely. Wait for them to answer. This silence is where the sale starts.
What happens next
+They say yes

"That is exactly what we do. We give you instant online booking so your clients can book you in 60 seconds. No forms. No waiting. Would you be open to a 20 minute call with our founder to see how it works for your business?"

–Not interested

"I hear you. Most operators I talk to say the same thing at first. Can I at least send you one quick email? No pressure at all."

?Pushes back on reviews

"Totally fair. Your service clearly speaks for itself. The booking piece is just what your own clients are bringing up. That is the only reason I called."

?Send me info first

"Of course. Before I do, would it be worth a quick 20 minute call with our founder so he can show you something specific to your setup? Much better than an email."

–Owner not available

"No problem. What is the best time to reach them? I will call back then."

Do NOT leave a message with staff. Call back at the exact time they give you.

–Goes to voicemail

"Hi [name], Sherwin from GridOps. I was on your Google reviews. Your clients love you. Quick question about your booking. Call me back at [your number] or book a time at gridops.ca"

Close the demo
Step 5
Book the demo
"Perfect. Let me send you a calendar link right now while I have you on the phone."
"It is a 20 minute call with Sam, our founder. He will show you exactly what it looks like for a company like yours."
"Did you get it? Great. You are all booked in. Sam will see you then."

If they hesitate on booking
"No pressure at all. Even just 20 minutes. Sam is going to show you something specific to your reviews and your business. Not a generic demo. If it is not relevant you can end the call early."
Two things to remember on every call The pause after "it is always about the booking experience" is the most important moment. Hold the silence even if it feels uncomfortable. The owner will fill it.

Always send the booking link while they are still on the phone. Never say "I will email it to you." Send it live and confirm they received it. That is the difference between a booked meeting and a maybe.
